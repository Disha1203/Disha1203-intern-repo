# Reflections

## Steps to handle data securely
1. Follow principle of least privilege
2. Use strong, unique passwords along with MFA
3. Don't share sensitive info through unsecured channels and only share it to authorised personal
4. Keep your devices and software updated
5. Be aware of phishing attacks
6. Make sure to follow company security policies 

## How to store, share and dispose sensitive data

### Storage
1. Use encrypted storage
2. Don't save sensitive data on personal devices
3. Store info only in company approved systems

### Sharing
1. Share using secure communication channels
2. Verify the recipients before sharing 
3. Share only necessary information

### Disposal
1. Follow company retention policies
2. Delete files when it's no longer needed

## How to respond to suspected data breach?
1.  Contain the breach by removing shared files, revoking access, etc
2. Inform to the  appropriate supervisor or security contact
3. Document the incident (what happened, info involved , people affected)
4. Cooperate with the investigation and follow company procedures

## What I learned
This task taught me that it is not just technicalities that are needed to safely keep things confidential; it is also responsibility. I learned more about the importance of storing, sharing and disposing confidential data in a secure manner, and the dangers of not doing so, such as phishing attacks, unauthorized access and accidental exposure.

I also learned that it is important to report any possible data breaches timely and abide by the company's security policies. In the future, I will ensure that I send information only after confirming with the recipient, use strong passwords with MFA and abide by the least privilege principle when accessing data.
